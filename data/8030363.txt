One is that it is close to the railway station, about 2 kilometers away, which is also the central area of ​​Haishu District. It is not far from Tianyi Square and Drum Tower; the other is that it is located at the end of the second phase of Nantang Old Street, a good place to be quiet in the bustling city.

It follows the unique architectural style of Jiangnan Ming and Qing residential buildings in Nantang Old Street, and deeply explores traditional architecture and folk culture. It faces the river on one side and the street on the other. On one side is the quiet river pier and on the other side is the street memory of the stone road and horse head wall. When you open the window lattice, combined with the small music played on the old street, you will suddenly feel a bit like "blue bricks with tile paint, white horses stepping on new mud". It also looks like a restaurant at night.

The koi pond and landscape trees at the entrance imitate nature and really feel like a private garden. The scenery changes with every step, and the corridor is winding, which has a sense of twists and turns. There are also children's areas, go couches, sofa areas, etc., which are poetic and picturesque and ingeniously designed.

The room type is better than the similar product Huilongwo Huajianfu where I stayed in Xuzhou. In particular, the bathroom has always been the shortcoming of Chinese B&Bs, but this one is quite spacious. The shower and bathtub are placed together, which will make the space larger. The room is also equipped with an air purifier, and the water and paper are quite generous.

The breakfast is a simple buffet + a good combination of meals. It is simple but well matched. There are also some good ones. For example, the meat in his wontons is very full, not the fake one with only a layer of skin, and the fried hairtail is better than what I have eaten in those restaurants on the old street. It’s a pity that guests from the north don’t appreciate this seafood.
